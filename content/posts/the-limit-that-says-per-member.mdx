I took over Cursor admin recently. They wanted someone to own the company's AI tooling.

The spend limit was already set: a per-member cap, multiplied by seat count. Standard admin hygiene for a dev team.

Then one member ran up on-demand charges alone. Far more than their share. Nearly the whole budget.

---

The UI said "per member." They read that as each person stops at their cap. That is the natural reading.

![Cursor team spend limit modal showing a fixed team limit with a "$100 per member" label beside the total](/images/posts/cursor-spend-limit.png)

Support clarified: the label is math, not enforcement. Per-member amount times seats equals one shared pool. Any single user can burn the whole thing before anyone gets blocked.

Per-user hard caps existed. Cursor told me they were deprecated on self-serve Team plans on December 5, 2025. Enterprise only now.

*The product looked safer for budget control than it was.*

---

Is this a dark pattern? Yes. The label creates a false affordance. The real behavior lives in small print below. Alerts notify you. They do not stop anyone. Turning off on-demand entirely is the only hard brake on self-serve.

---

I turned off on-demand while figuring out what to do next.

I build interfaces for a living. I know what "per member" implies. Cursor knows too.

The limit that says per member does not limit per member.
